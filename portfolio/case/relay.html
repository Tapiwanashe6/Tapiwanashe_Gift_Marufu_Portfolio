( 01 ) Context

We all hate standups. We're all still doing them. Why?

I interviewed three distributed teams (8, 14 and 21 people). All three complained about meeting fatigue. All three still held a daily standup. The real blocker wasn't meetings; it was a lack of a place for async updates to live, close enough to the work that people would actually read them.

( 02 ) The bet

Relay's bet is that a three-minute video note, posted once a day, against a specific project, replaces 90% of the standup. The remaining 10% is worth a real call.
