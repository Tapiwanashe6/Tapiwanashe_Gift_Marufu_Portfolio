Pulse — a quieter way to track your health.

( 01 ) Context

Existing trackers treat chronic patients like athletes. Pulse treats them like humans.

We spoke with 14 people managing long-term conditions — from Type 2 diabetes to rheumatoid arthritis. A pattern emerged quickly: the apps they'd been prescribed felt like homework. Streaks, red warnings, graphs that only made sense to their doctor.

Our design brief was deliberately soft: build a daily ritual that the patient wants to return to, and produce a once-a-week artefact the clinician can scan in under 30 seconds.

( 02 ) Research

14 patient interviews, 4 clinician ride-alongs, and a two-week diary study. Three findings reshaped the product:

The weekly clinician-facing artefact became the north star — if that wasn't useful, nothing else mattered.

Colour was used to separate data from emotion. The app itself reads almost entirely in neutrals; the only tinted element is the clinician export, which uses a restrained traffic-light for at-a-glance reading.
