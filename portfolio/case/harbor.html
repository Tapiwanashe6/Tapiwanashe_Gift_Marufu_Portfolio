( 01 ) Context

A journaling app shouldn't shout at you for skipping Tuesday.

I started Harbor after watching a friend delete three "mood tracker" apps in a month. Every one of them punished absence: red dots on calendars, "you missed 2 days" banners, streak flames. None of that is how grief, or burnout, or a hard week actually works.

( 02 ) Design principles

No
streaks, ever
3
sentences default length
1
gentle evening nudge per day

The heatmap — a staple of habit apps — is recontextualised: squares never turn "red" for missed days. They simply stay empty, the same neutral grey, and the surrounding copy speaks to presence, not absence.
